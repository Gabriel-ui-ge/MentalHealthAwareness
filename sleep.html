Why Building Healthy Sleep Habits is Important

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention(CDC) highligths how notability of healthy sleep habits for the youth. Teenagers require 8-10 hours of sleep a night for optimal health. Due to school demand, scholarly stress, extracurriculars, and electronic usage, most fall short. The CDC reports that an insufficient amount of sleep correlates with high levels of stress, decreased academic performance, and increased depression and anxiety. Establishing a regular sleep schedule, limiting electronic usage before bed, and resting in an environment catered to you, teenagers can significantly improve their mental and emotional health.
